About me and this site

If you landed here it probably means you want to know a bit more about who I am or what this site is all about. Let me try to answer those questions.

The short version

I'm a developer and designer based in a small town. I've been building things for the web since the early 2010s and writing on this blog since 2017. If you want to chat, you can reach me at hello@example.com.

The longer version

I grew up in a quiet part of the world, spent most of my school years being fascinated by computers and the internet. After trying a few different paths in education, I eventually found my way to web development and never looked back. There's something about building things for the web that feels right — the openness, the accessibility, the creative freedom.

I started freelancing shortly after and have been working independently ever since. It's not always easy — the freedom comes with uncertainty, and the flexibility comes with a tendency to never truly stop working. But I wouldn't trade it for the predictability of a typical office job. At least, not most days.

Outside of work, I'm drawn to nature, mountains, and quiet places. Cities have their charm but I find myself happiest when surrounded by trees and open sky. One day I'd love to have a small place somewhere remote, with enough space to breathe and think.

I share my life with a dog who is simultaneously the best and worst decision I've ever made. He's large, stubborn, and endlessly entertaining.

Online presence

I stepped away from social media a while ago and don't miss it. My writing lives here on this blog, and occasionally I share things on Are.na. I believe in owning your own content and keeping things simple. The web is better when people have their own spaces rather than renting attention on platforms.

About this site

I designed and built this site myself. It's intentionally minimal — no analytics, no trackers, no unnecessary JavaScript. Just words, images, and the occasional link. The typeface is Iowan, a beautiful serif that's easy on the eyes for long reading sessions.

The site is always a work in progress. I enjoy tweaking small details, adjusting spacing, and refining the reading experience. It's as much a creative outlet as the writing itself.

Get in touch

I'm a big fan of email. My inbox is always open and I enjoy hearing from people. Whether you have a question about my work, want to discuss an idea, or just want to say hello — feel free to reach out at hello@example.com.
